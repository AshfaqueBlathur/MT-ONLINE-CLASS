Oxy-acetylene
welding



In this
process, the heat source is combustion of acetylene gas.

Chemical
reaction
of acetylene and oxygen produces a great deal of heat.


The oxyacetylene
flame burns with temperature exceeding 3500Â°C,
enough to melt most metals and alloys.






There are two types of systems for
oxy-acetylene welding



      1. High pressure
system:


  Here both oxygen
and acetylene gases are drawn from   cylinders in
which these gases are stored at high pressure.



     
2. Low pressure system:

   In this
system, oxygen gas is drawn from a cylinder as   before, but
acetylene gas is produced at site at low   pressure.
